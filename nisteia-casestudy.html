The Problem

Orthodox fasting has no good digital guide

Greek Orthodox fasting during Holy Week isn't one simple rule. It changes every day. Fish is allowed on Palm Sunday but not Good Friday. Oil and wine are fine on Holy Thursday but off the table on Wednesday. People who've grown up with this know it by heart. But two groups are really struggling.

Older community members

A lot of older parishioners learned the rules from their parents. But when that gets lost through distance, diaspora, or just life getting in the way, there's nowhere simple to turn. No app they can actually use comfortably.

Younger Greeks reconnecting

More and more young Greek Australians want to participate in Holy Week but have no idea where to start. They didn't grow up with it, feel embarrassed asking, and end up not fasting at all.

In short
There's no app built specifically for Orthodox fasting. Especially not one that works for someone who is new to it.
The Solution

Νηστεία: five tabs, one purpose each

Nisteia is a clean, simple mobile app. The whole idea is that you open it and immediately know what to do. White cards, big readable text, a green scan button. No clutter, no learning curve.

☀️
Tab 1

Today

You see the fasting rules for the day straight away. A strip of Holy Week days across the top so you can tap between them. A simple grid showing what's allowed and what's not. Meat, dairy, fish, oil, wine. Green tick or red cross. Plus a short plain-English note explaining what the day actually means.

📷
Tab 2

Scan

Scan any product barcode and find out instantly if you can eat it today. It pulls from the Open Food Facts database, which is free and doesn't need an API key. The result is big and obvious: "You can eat this!" or "Avoid this today", with a simple reason if it's not allowed.

🍽️
Tab 3

Recipes

A collection of traditional Greek fasting recipes. Fasolada, Fakes, Taramasalata, Lagana, Spanakorizo, Halva and more. Each one has the Greek name, how long it takes, difficulty, and numbered steps. The recipes automatically filter to only show what you're allowed to make that day.

📖
Tab 4

Guide

This is for anyone who's never done this before. What is fasting? What can I eat? Do I have to be perfect? Why is Good Friday the hardest day? Simple FAQ cards you tap to open. Warm and straightforward, no assumptions about what you already know.

✨
Tab 5

For You

You pick the ingredients and foods you enjoy and the app builds personalised meal and snack ideas around them. It checks what's allowed on today's fasting rules first, then uses AI to put something together that actually suits you. Not just a list of random recipes — suggestions that feel like they were made for you. Great for people who know what they like but have no idea how to make it work during Holy Week.

Who It's For

Designed for two very different people

The young reconnector

20s, Greek-Australian, curious about Orthodoxy. Has never properly fasted before. Wants to try Holy Week fasting but has no idea where to start and feels embarrassed to ask family. Needs clear rules, simple language, and recipe ideas they can actually cook.

1

Opens the app. Sees the Good Friday rules. Big grid, almost everything red except vegetables and legumes.

2

Taps the Guide tab and reads the FAQ: "Do I have to be perfect?" The answer is no. Do what you can.

3

Scans the yoghurt in the fridge. App says "Avoid this today" and shows the reason: contains dairy.

4

Opens Recipes and follows the steps for Lentil Soup (Fakes). Makes dinner.

The experienced parish elder

60s or 70s, devout, has fasted their whole life. Knows the tradition well but wants something to double-check a product at the supermarket. Needs big text, simple navigation, a clear yes or no without any fuss, and the option to use the app entirely in Greek.

1

Opens the app on Holy Thursday morning. Sees the tip that oil and wine are allowed today.

2

Standing in the supermarket. Taps Scan and enters the barcode on a packet of crackers.

3

"You can eat this!" Big green tick. Shows their grandchild standing next to them.

4

Shares the Recipes tab with the grandkids. Passes the tradition on in a way that actually sticks.

Design Principles

Built to be understood by anyone

Simplicity first

Every screen has one job. No clutter, no hidden menus. You should know what to do within a few seconds of opening it.

Plain language always

No church jargon, no assumptions. If a rule exists, it's explained in plain English. What it means for your actual meal.

Inclusive of all ages

Big tap targets, generous text sizes, high contrast. Works just as well for a 70-year-old yiayia as it does for a 19-year-old student.

Encouraging, not guilt-based

The Guide makes it clear: you don't have to be perfect. The app meets you where you're at and supports whatever effort you're making.

Clean and scannable

White cards, green accents, big verdicts. The design makes answers feel immediate. You should never have to hunt for information.

Culturally grounded

Greek names sit alongside English. The cross, the tradition, the recipes — it all feels genuinely Orthodox, not generic.

In short
Scan it, check it, cook it. No background knowledge required.
Core Features

What the MVP includes

Feature 01

Daily fasting rules

Rules for every day of Holy Week. Meat, dairy, fish, shellfish, oil and wine. Green tick or red cross. Plus a short note explaining what the day means.

Feature 02

Barcode scanner

Scan any product and get an instant yes or no. Powered by the Open Food Facts database, free to use.

Feature 03

Recipe library

Traditional Greek fasting recipes with step-by-step instructions. Only shows what's allowed for the day you've selected.

Feature 04

Beginner's guide

A simple FAQ for first-time fasters. What is fasting? What can I eat? Why is Good Friday the hardest? All answered clearly.

Feature 05

Strictness toggle

Not everyone fasts the same way and that's okay. You can choose between lenient, standard and strict to match how you observe.

Feature 06

Score ring

Each scanned product gets a compliance score from 0 to 100. A quick visual read so you know exactly where it sits.

Feature 07

Greek language support

A simple toggle to switch the entire app between English and Greek. Every screen, every label, every fasting verdict switches over. Built so older community members who are more comfortable in Greek can use the app fully without any friction.

Feature 08

Personalised meal suggestions

You tell the app what foods you enjoy and it puts together fasting-friendly meal and snack ideas built around your preferences. It takes into account what's actually allowed on that day, so every suggestion is one you can eat right now. Powered by AI so the results feel personal, not generic.

Post-MVP ideas: push notifications, offline mode, community recipes, year-round fasting calendar.

Technical Approach

Built by Aaron, assisted by AI

Aaron is building this himself. He'll set up the project, create the files and manage the codebase end to end. AI will come in to help with specific logic, API integration and tricky styling bits, but the goal is to own every decision.

FrontendReact Native for mobile or Next.js as a PWA. Decision made at project start.
StylingTailwind CSS. Utility-first and consistent with existing projects.
DatabasePostgreSQL via Prisma ORM. For recipes, user data and scan history.
Barcode APIOpen Food Facts. Open source, free, no API key needed, community maintained.
Localisationi18next. The standard library for multilingual React apps. English and Greek JSON files, language toggled by the user in settings.
Meal AIClaude API (Anthropic). Takes the user's food preferences and today's fasting rules, returns personalised meal and snack suggestions on the fly.
DeploymentVercel for web, Expo for mobile.
AI AssistClaude by Anthropic. Code suggestions, debugging and content help during the build.
AuthClerk or NextAuth.js. Post-MVP, for user accounts and saved recipes.
Future Vision

Starting with Holy Week, growing with the calendar

The plan is to start with Holy Week and grow from there. The Greek Orthodox calendar has fasting periods throughout the whole year and the same core features work for all of them. Barcode scanning, daily rules, recipes. It all applies.

  • Expand to cover the Apostles' Fast, Dormition Fast, Nativity Fast and the regular Wednesday and Friday fasting
  • Full Greek language support so older first-generation community members can use the app entirely in Greek, reviewed by a native speaker for accuracy
  • A community recipe library where people can submit their own family recipes
  • A personal fasting journal to track your observance across the year
  • Push notifications at the start of each fasting period
  • Potential partnerships with Greek Orthodox parishes and community groups in Australia
  • A lightweight web version for people on desktop or tablet
Long-term goal
To make Νηστεία the go-to resource for Orthodox fasting in the Australian Greek community, and eventually beyond.
